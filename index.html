üéØ Core Philosophy

  • Simple Turns & Clear Choices: Each turn offers Move, Attack, Special Power, and Enhancement‚Äîeasy to grasp and fast to play.
  • Visual Progress: Track HP with crossed-off circles, draw maps, and mark quests in an adventure log for tangible feedback.
  • Fast & Engaging Combat: Early fights finish in 3‚Äì6 rounds, epic bosses in up to 10‚Äîkeeps energy high and decisions impactful.
  • Clear Success & Immediate Rewards: Rolls: 10+ normal, 15+ hard, beat AC for attacks. Celebrate wins with vivid descriptions and instant treasure or story advancement.
  • Gradual Complexity & Meaningful Choices: Introduce new abilities slowly across levels so each level-up feels rewarding without overwhelm.
  • Kid-Friendly Features: Abilities are once-per-adventure or simple recharge. Failure leads to story twists, never dead ends.

üë§ Character Creation

Players choose one of five hero archetypes with preset stats for quick balance. Then personalize with:

  • Appearance: Height, hair style/color, eye color, special attire.
  • Personality: Brave, cautious, silly, serious, talkative, observant.
  • Backstory: One or two sentences: origin, favorite animal, main goal (e.g., ‚Äúfind lost treasure‚Äù).
  • Favorites: Color, food, pet‚Äîsmall details make characters memorable.

‚öîÔ∏è Simplified Combat Rules

Basic Turn Structure

  1. Move: Up to Speed in squares. Diagonals = 1 square. Difficult terrain = 2 squares per move.
  2. Attack: Roll 1d20 + Attack Bonus vs AC. Describe hits or creative misses.
  3. Special Power: Use your class‚Äôs unique ability (once/turn or once/adventure as noted).
  4. Enhancement: Use one active ability from your level-up choices.

Easy Success Numbers

  • Normal Task: Roll 10+ on 1d20.
  • Hard Task: Roll 15+ on 1d20.
  • Attack: 1d20 + Attack Bonus vs AC.
  • Natural 20: Critical hit = double damage.

Damage & Healing

  • Track HP as circles; cross off when damaged.
  • At 0 HP, hero is defeated (unconscious) but returns next round at half HP.
  • Healing: potions, abilities, or spirit magic restore crossed-off HP circles.

Narrative Simplification

  • Clear Motivation: ‚ÄúSave the village,‚Äù ‚Äúfind treasure,‚Äù ‚Äústop the villain.‚Äù

üé§ DM Guidelines

Managing the Table

  • Clear turn order: index cards or clockwise announcements.
  • Encourage creativity: ask ‚ÄúHow?‚Äù not just ‚ÄúWhat?‚Äù
  • Keep energy high: voices, cheers, dynamic descriptions.
  • Quick rulings: Fair ruling on the spot, look up details later.

When Things Go Wrong

  • Too easy? Add reinforcements or dramatic twists.
  • Too hard? Enemies flee, trip, or unexpected allies arrive.
  • Players stuck? Give obvious hints after 1‚Äì2 minutes.
  • Defeated heroes return next round at half HP‚Äîfun, not frustration.

Success Measurements

  • Players engaged and ask questions.
  • Combats end in under 10 rounds.
  • Everyone uses their special power.
  • Story advances every 15 minutes.
  • Players exclaim, ‚ÄúThat was awesome!‚Äù

Managing Complexity

  • Introduce one new mechanic per level.
  • Use reference cards for complex abilities.
  • Let players specialize in a few powers.
  • Keep core resolution: 1d20 + bonus vs target number.

Keeping It Kid-Friendly

  • Power fantasy: describe epic new abilities.
  • Failure = plot twist, never dead end.
  • Celebrate level-ups, milestones, loot ceremonies.
  • Visual progression: character sheets, hall of heroes board.

Long-Term Campaign Tips

  • Document adventures in a shared log or journal.
  • Recurring NPCs who evolve with heroes‚Äô reputations.
  • Bards and statues commemorate epic deeds.
  • At retirement, heroes guide new adventurers or rule realms.

  3. Special Power: use if ready.
